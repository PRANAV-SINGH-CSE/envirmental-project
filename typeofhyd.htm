Types of Hybrid Seeds


Wheat

The female wheat plants in a field are pollinated by male plants of a different line, with the goal of producing seeds that carry stronger yield potential and adaptability to adverse environments than either parent.
The fertilized female plant produces new, unique offspring called a hybrid.


Rice

Hybrid rice is produced when the egg is fertilized by pollen from anthers of a rice plant from a different variety or line.
In order to produce great quantities of hybrid seeds, two kinds of parental lines are needed, a seed parent which is usually male sterile and a pollen parent.


Cauliflower

There are many different hybrid varieties of Cauliflower.

Orange cauliflower: It is a hybridized variety first discovered in Canada in 1970, containing more beta carotene and making it a good source of vitamin A.

Green cauliflower: It is also a hybrid variety between cauliflower and broccoli. The hybrid is also sometimes called Broccoflower.


Mustard

There are many different hybrid varieties of Mustard. These are adapted to the temperate climates of Europe and to mild winters in various regions, producing high-quality, firm curds weighing 1-1.5 kgs.
